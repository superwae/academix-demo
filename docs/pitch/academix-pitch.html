The Problem

MENA's online learners and teachers deserve better tools.

Global LMS platforms are English-first, built on Western payment rails, and ignore the realities of how Arabic-speaking users, institutions, and employers actually work.

!

No Arabic-native UX

Udemy and Coursera are written LTR-first. Arabic content is a second-class citizen — no right-to-left layout, no native Arabic UX, no Arabic instructor tooling.

₪

Local payment rails ignored

Lahza, MyFatoorah, HyperPay — the gateways 250M+ people actually use — are not supported. Teachers lose 5–10% per sale to forex + processor fees, and some students simply can't check out at all.

⚙

Institutions stitch it together

Schools and companies today run Zoom + Google Forms + spreadsheets + a WhatsApp group. Every institution is reinventing the same broken workflow.

✕

No platform serves all three sides

Independent teachers, teaching institutions, and corporate L&D each get a half-built tool or a misaligned product. Nothing scales from one teacher to a 10,000-seat enterprise.

The gap: a modern, Arabic-native, MENA-payment-native LMS that scales from a single teacher to an enterprise-wide training program.
